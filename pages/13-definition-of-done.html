DEFINITION OF DONE

In agile development, each Increment shapes future progress. Poor quality in one Sprint can hinder productivity. As each Sprint draws to a close, the team's focus shifts to delivering Increments that meet the criteria of "Done," a term that can vary in interpretation among team members.
To mitigate ambiguity and foster a shared understanding of completion, Scrum Teams establish a Definition of Done (DoD). A strong DoD is vital for the success of Scrum, ensuring a balance between speed and quality.

WHAT IS DoD

The DoD, as the name implies, defines the quality standards an Increment must meet to be considered finished. It is a formal description of the state of the Increment when it meets the quality measures required for the product. The moment a Product Backlog item (PBI) meets the DoD, an Increment is born. DoD should not be confused with User Story Acceptance Criteria. The Acceptance Criteria are specific to individual stories. But the DoD encapsulates the overall quality expectations for the entire Increment.

WHO DEFINES THE DoD

The DoD is closely tied to quality and is set by the development organization. Established organizations maintain standards, conventions, and guidelines to regulate their work, ensuring goal achievement. Every project or initiative must align with these standards. In some cases, the organization's quality standards align with external benchmarks. If it is not an organizational standard, the Scrum Team must create a DoD appropriate for the product. If there are multiple Scrum Teams working together on a product, they must mutually define and comply with the same DoD.

PURPOSE OF DoD

  • Creates transparency by providing everyone a shared understanding of what work was completed as part of the Increment.
  • Guides the Developers on how many PBIs to select for the Sprint.
  • Leads to higher quality, increased value, and reduced technical debt in each Increment.

CHARACTERISTICS OF DoD

  • A Short, Measurable Checklist: The DoD should consist of items that are concise, measurable, and testable, allowing for clear verification of completion. The criteria might also be related to architecture, non-functional/system requirements, process management, configuration management, performance, security, maintainability, and usability and so on.
  • Reflect Shippable Quality: It should mirror shippable quality, ensuring that the product increment meets the necessary standards for release.
  • Agreed Upon by the Team: The DoD should be a shared understanding among the entire Scrum Team, and other stakeholders.
  • Continuous Improvement: The DoD should be subject to continuous reflection and improvement, with the team revisiting and updating it regularly to align with evolving project needs and quality standards.

WHO SHOULD FOLLOW THE DoD

  • The Developers are required to conform to the DoD when developing an increment.
  • If the DoD for an increment is part of the standards of the organization, all Scrum Teams must follow it as a minimum.
  • If there are multiple Scrum Teams collaborating on a product, they may choose to apply a more stringent DoD within their own teams, but must adhere to at least the agreed-upon criteria for the Increment.

WHAT HAPPENS WHEN DoD IS NOT MET

If a PBI does not meet the DoD, it cannot be released or even presented at the Sprint Review. Instead, it returns to the Product Backlog for future consideration.

WHEN TO REVIEW THE DoD

The DoD should not be considered a static document, but should undergo regular reviews and adjustments based on the evolving insights of the Scrum Team. It should evolve as the team's knowledge of the product grows and as the product itself evolves.
The Sprint Retrospective is the ideal occasion to assess and refine the DoD. Therefore, introducing new criteria may lead the team to realize the need for additional work in previously completed Increments. Nonetheless, teams can discuss and modify the DoD more frequently if needed.
The crucial aspect is to foster open communication within the team to ensure that the DoD accurately reflects the necessary criteria for building a usable and potentially releasable Increment.
