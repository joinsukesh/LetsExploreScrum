SCRUM MASTER

The Scrum Master (SM) is accountable for establishing Scrum as defined in the Scrum Guide. They do this by helping everyone understand Scrum theory and practice, both within the Scrum Team and the organization.
The SM is accountable for the Scrum Team's effectiveness. They do this by enabling the Scrum Team to improve its practices, within the Scrum framework.

RESPONSIBILITIES

Helps the Scrum Team

The SM serves the Scrum Team in several ways, including:

 Coaching the team members in self-management and cross-functionality
  • The SM helps team members understand and embody the principles of self-management, encouraging them to take ownership of their work, collaborate effectively without constant supervision, and adapt to changing requirements autonomously.
  • They facilitate discussions and workshops to promote cross-functionality within the team, ensuring that team members have a diverse skill set and can collectively tackle any task required to deliver the Increment.
 Helping the Scrum Team focus on creating high-value Increments that meet the Definition of Done (DoD)
  • The SM works with the Product Owner and the team to ensure a clear understanding of what constitutes a "high-value Increment" based on the Product Backlog and stakeholder feedback.
  • They facilitate discussions during Sprint Planning to ensure that the team selects user stories or backlog items that contribute to the overall product goals and deliver value to the stakeholders.
  • Throughout the Sprint, the SM helps the team stay focused on delivering a potentially shippable product Increment that meets the DoD, ensuring that each item completed meets the quality standards set by the team.
 Causing the removal of impediments to the Scrum Team's progress
  • The SM actively identifies obstacles or impediments that hinder the team's progress, whether they are related to organizational issues, technical challenges, or interpersonal conflicts.
  • They work closely with the team to address these impediments, either by facilitating discussions to find solutions collaboratively or by escalating issues to management or other stakeholders when necessary.
  • By removing impediments promptly, the SM enables the team to maintain their focus and momentum, ensuring that they can deliver value consistently throughout the Sprint.
 Ensuring that all Scrum events take place and are positive, productive, and kept within the timebox
  • The SM acts as a facilitator for all Scrum events, ensuring that they are scheduled, organized, and conducted effectively.
  • They set the stage for each event, creating a positive and inclusive environment where team members feel comfortable sharing their ideas and concerns.
  • During the events, the SM keeps the team focused on the agenda and ensures that discussions remain relevant to the goals of the event.
  • They also enforce timeboxing to ensure that each event is kept within the allocated time frame, helping the team maintain a cadence and rhythm throughout the Sprint.

Helps the Product Owner

The SM serves the Product Owner in several ways, including:

 Helping find techniques for effective Product Goal definition and Product Backlog management
  • The SM assists the Product Owner in defining a clear and compelling Product Goal, which serves as the overarching objective for the Scrum Team. This involves understanding the market, customer needs, and business objectives to articulate a vision that aligns with the organization's goals.
  • Additionally, the SM helps the Product Owner in managing the Product Backlog effectively. This includes prioritizing items based on value, dependencies, and risks.
 Helping the Scrum Team understand the need for clear and concise Product Backlog items

Clear items facilitate better understanding and alignment among team members, while concise items help in maintaining focus and reducing ambiguity.

  • The SM educates the Scrum Team on the importance of having clear, concise, and actionable Product Backlog items.
    This involves coaching the team on writing user stories effectively, breaking down larger initiatives into smaller, manageable tasks, and ensuring that each backlog item has clear acceptance criteria.
 Helping establish empirical product planning for a complex environment

Empirical planning relies on transparency, inspection, and adaptation to make informed decisions based on real-world data and feedback.

  • The SM helps the team use flexible planning methods to deal with uncertainties in their project by facilitating activities like Sprint Planning, where the team works together to estimate what they can finish in the next Sprint based on their abilities and the tasks they need to do.
  • The SM also assists the team in adjusting their plans based on feedback from past Sprints and changes in the market, so the product can improve gradually and meet new needs effectively.
 Facilitating stakeholder collaboration as requested or needed
  • The SM acts as a facilitator to foster collaboration between the Scrum Team and stakeholders, including customers, users, sponsors, and other relevant parties. This may involve organizing meetings, workshops, or demos to gather feedback, clarify requirements, and align expectations.
  • The SM helps in ensuring that stakeholders are engaged throughout the development process, providing valuable insights and validation to the team's work.
  • Additionally, the SM helps in resolving conflicts and managing expectations between stakeholders and the Scrum Team, ensuring that communication channels remain open and productive.

Supports the Organization

The SM serves the organization in several ways, including:

 Leading, training, and coaching the organization in its Scrum adoption
  • The SM plays a crucial role in guiding the organization through the process of adopting Scrum principles and practices. This involves not only understanding the fundamentals of Scrum themselves but also effectively communicating these principles to others within the organization.
  • They provide training sessions and workshops to help individuals and teams understand the Scrum framework, its roles, events, and artifacts.
  • Coaching involves providing ongoing support and guidance to individuals and teams as they navigate the challenges of implementing Scrum in their day-to-day work.
 Helping employees and stakeholders understand and enact an empirical approach for complex work
  • The SM educates employees and stakeholders on the empirical nature of Scrum and how it provides a framework for dealing with complex and uncertain work.
  • They help individuals and teams embrace the principles of transparency, inspection, and adaptation, encouraging them to continuously assess their work and adapt their processes accordingly.
  • They facilitate discussions and activities that promote a culture of experimentation and learning, encouraging teams to take calculated risks and learn from both successes and failures.
 Removing barriers between stakeholders and Scrum Teams
  • The SM acts as a servant-leader, working to eliminate obstacles and impediments that may hinder the progress of Scrum Teams.
  • They serve as a liaison between stakeholders and development teams, ensuring clear communication and alignment of goals and expectations.
  • They identify and address issues related to resource constraints, organizational politics, conflicting priorities, and other factors that may impact the ability of teams to deliver value effectively.
  • They foster collaboration and cooperation across departments and functions, helping to create an environment where teams can thrive and deliver high-quality products and services.
