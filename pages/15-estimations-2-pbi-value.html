ESTIMATIONS - BACKLOG ITEM VALUE

Defining the value of a Product Backlog item in Scrum is crucial for effective prioritization and decision-making throughout the development process. The Scrum Guide does not prescribe any technique for this purpose, and it is up to the Product Owner to define which technique to use for this purpose. One such technique is the Kano Model.

THE KANO MODEL

The Kano Model is a theory developed by Professor Noriaki Kano in the 1980s to understand and prioritize customer preferences in product development. It categorizes features into three main types:

 Basic Needs: These are essential features that customers expect as a minimum requirement. They don't necessarily delight customers when present, but their absence can lead to dissatisfaction.
Examples of such features in a smartphone might include basic functionality like making calls, sending text messages, and connecting to the internet.

 Performance Needs: These are features that directly correlate with customer satisfaction. The more of these features a product has, the more satisfied customers are. However, there's diminishing returnsâ€”adding more of these features doesn't necessarily lead to increased satisfaction.
Examples of such features in a smartphone might include battery life, processing speed, or screen resolution.

 Excitement Needs: These are unexpected or innovative features that can delight customers and significantly increase their satisfaction when present, but their absence doesn't lead to dissatisfaction.
Examples of such features in a smartphone might include a built-in virtual assistant, innovative camera features, or customizable user interfaces.

The Kano Model suggests that customer needs evolve over time, so what was once considered an excitement need can become a performance need and eventually a basic need. Understanding and adapting to these changing needs is crucial for maintaining customer satisfaction and staying competitive in the market.

HOW DO WE USE KANO MODEL TO ORDER THE PRODUCT BACKLOG ITEMS

In Scrum Product Backlog refinement, the Kano Model can be applied during the prioritization of backlog items. Here's how:

 Identify Feature Types: When refining the product backlog, the team can categorize backlog items into these three types based on customer feedback, market analysis, and product vision.

 Prioritize Backlog Items: Prioritize backlog items based on their Kano type. Basic needs should be addressed first to ensure customer satisfaction and avoid dissatisfaction. Then, focus on performance needs to improve satisfaction further. Finally, incorporate excitement needs to delight customers and differentiate the product.

 Iterative Refinement: As the product evolves and more feedback is gathered, continue refining the backlog items based on their Kano types. This ensures that the product remains aligned with customer preferences and market demands.


Understanding these categories can help product teams prioritize features more effectively, leading to higher customer satisfaction and a more successful product.
