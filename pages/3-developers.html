DEVELOPERS

Developers are the people in the Scrum Team that are committed to creating any aspect of a usable Increment each Sprint.
The term "Developers" is not used in the narrow sense of those who write code, but in the broad sense of those who develop the product. This could include activities such as design, coding, testing, etc. Scrum uses the term "Developers" not to exclude but to simplify.
The specific skills needed by the Developers are often broad, and will vary with the domain of work. In Scrum, the Developers are cross-functional. In other words, the sum of the skills of all the Developers must be enough to deliver all the aspects of a usable product.

Each Developer should have the flexibility to take on tasks based on their skills, expertise, and capacity during the Sprint.
A Developer never becomes solely accountable for the value of a Product Backlog item selected for the Sprint. The Developers collectively own the work, and collaborate to complete the Sprint Backlog items, and to achieve the Sprint Goal.

RESPONSIBILITIES

The Developers are always accountable for:

 Creating a plan for the Sprint (Sprint Backlog)
  • The Developers are responsible for the estimation of effort required to complete a Product Backlog item. This information helps in capacity planning and determining the Sprint Backlog and Sprint Goal during Sprint Planning.
  • They actively participate in Sprint Planning to establish a Sprint Backlog, which includes defining the Sprint Goal, selecting the Product Backlog items that they will work on, and creating a plan for completing these items, including a Definition of Done.
    This self-selection process reinforces their autonomy and allows them to commit to a realistic and achievable scope of work for each Sprint, based on their understanding of their capacity, and the Product Owner's input on value and priorities.
  • Developers are responsible for managing the Sprint Backlog. This involves breaking down user stories or tasks into smaller, manageable pieces.
  • It's up to the Developers to see how many items they can develop during the Sprint.
  • Ultimately, the Developers have the autonomy and authority to make decisions related to the Sprint Backlog, and the implementation of the work.
 Instilling quality by adhering to a Definition of Done (DoD)
  • Developers conform to the DoD, and ensure that the product increment meets the quality standards set by the team. The DoD outlines the criteria for an increment to be considered complete. It encompasses aspects like coding standards, testing, documentation, and integration.
 Adapting their plan each day toward the Sprint Goal
  • They engage in Daily Scrums everyday to discuss progress, challenges, and adjustments needed to achieve the Sprint Goal, and adapt their plan based on emerging information, and impediments. This includes managing the Sprint Backlog, and updating it everyday to reflect the remaining work.
 Holding each other accountable as professionals
  • The Developers are accountable for creating a plan, and organizing their work, while also collaborating with the Product Owner, and the Scrum Master as needed.
  • They self-organize to determine the best approach for accomplishing the work planned for the Sprint in the most efficient manner.
  • They collaborate closely, to ensure transparency in managing, and delivering the Sprint Backlog.
  • They collectively address any roadblocks, and support one another to achieve the Sprint Goal.
 Embracing Scrum Values
  • Courage: Address conflicts within the team openly, and work together to resolve them.
  • Openness: Be receptive to each other's ideas, and viewpoints.
  • Focus: Stay committed to the work, and avoid distractions.
  • Commitment: Strive to create a "Done" increment.
  • Respect: Value all team members, and their contributions.
 Developers may also take on additional responsibilities
  • Facilitate team events like the Daily Scrum.
  • Interact with stakeholders, to gain a better understanding of their needs, and to gather feedback on the product.
  • Developers with specialized skills can mentor or coach other team members.
  • Share knowledge, and learn from each other.
