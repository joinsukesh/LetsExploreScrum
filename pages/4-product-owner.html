PRODUCT OWNER

A Product Owner (PO) plays a crucial role in ensuring the success of a product.
The PO is accountable for maximizing the value of the product resulting from the work of the Scrum Team. How this is done may vary widely across organizations, Scrum Teams, and individuals.

RESPONSIBILITIES

The PO is also accountable for effective Product Backlog management, which includes:

 Developing, and explicitly communicating the Product Goal
  • The PO is responsible for defining, and articulating a clear Product Goal that outlines the overarching purpose, and objectives of the product.
    This involves understanding the needs of the stakeholders, market trends, and business objectives to create a vision for what the product should achieve.
  • The Product Goal serves as a guiding light for the development team, helping them understand the broader context, and direction of their work. The PO communicates effectively the Product Goal, to all stakeholders, including the development team, to ensure alignment, and focus on achieving the desired outcomes.
 Creating, and clearly communicating Product Backlog items

Product Backlog items should be granular enough to be completed within a single Sprint, and provide tangible value to the product.

  • The PO is responsible for breaking down the requirements, and features of the product into actionable Product Backlog items. This involves writing clear, and concise user stories or backlog items that describe the functionality, user interactions, and acceptance criteria.
  • The PO communicates these items to the development team during Sprint Planning meetings, clarifies any ambiguities or questions that arise, providing additional context when needed, and ensures that they have a shared understanding of what needs to be implemented.
 Ordering Product Backlog items
  • Prioritizing the Product Backlog is a crucial responsibility of the PO.
    They must consider various factors such as business value, market demand, technical dependencies, and risk when ordering the backlog items.
    High-priority items that deliver the most value or mitigate significant risks should be placed at the top of the Product Backlog.
  • The PO regularly reviews, and adjusts the priority of backlog items based on changing requirements, feedback from stakeholders, and the team's capacity.
 Ensuring that the Product Backlog is transparent, visible, and understood.

Transparency is key to the success of Scrum, and the PO plays a vital role in ensuring that the Product Backlog is transparent to all stakeholders.

  • The PO maintains the Product Backlog in a visible, and accessible format, such as a digital tool or physical board, so that everyone involved can see the current status, and priorities.
  • The PO communicates changes to the backlog, such as new items, updates, or reprioritizations, to the development team, and stakeholders promptly.

The PO may do the above work or may delegate the responsibility to others. Regardless, the PO remains accountable.
For POs to succeed, the entire organization must respect their decisions. These decisions are visible in the content, and ordering of the Product Backlog, and through the inspectable Increment at the Sprint Review.
The PO is one person, not a committee. The PO may represent the needs of many stakeholders in the Product Backlog. Those wanting to change the Product Backlog can do so by trying to convince the PO.
